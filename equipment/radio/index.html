Everything to do with radio transmission and reception

Radios, TX modules, receivers, antennas, and any other misc accessories for radios, all you need to get control over your craft.
If you're trying to decide, or don't even have any prior experience, this list will hopefully help you!
Prices may depend on where and when you source the parts, (prices and specs are sourced from the manufacturers website when possible)

Radios

Couldn't fly without one if you don't count throwing the quad in the air. You will find all options from budget to high-end, from small gamepad style radios to full size ones, and all inbetween

Jumper T-Lite

Price: $85/$75

TX: 4in1/CC2500

Gimbals: Hall

Weight: 260g

It's been the best budget option for a while, as it was about the cheapest you could've gotten a multiprotocol radio. But now it's getting harder to find as Jumper seem to be pushing it off in favor of their newer radios

Radiomaster TX12

Price: $89.99

TX: CC2500

Gimbals: Potentiometer

Weight: 325g

Now for radios you might be able to get your hands on. The TX12 is very similar to the T12 line, because Radiomaster used to be a part of Jumper. For it's lower price, it loses the full multiproto functions, and you only have pot gimbals, however this isn't too big of an issue

Radiomaster TX16S

Price: $175.99

TX: 4in1

Gimbals: Hall

Weight: 736g

This is pretty much the best radio you can get your hands on for the price. When it came out it used to cost about $130, but now it's gotten way up. But still, the color touch screen, wide range of possible upgrades, and expansion space make it the most universal radio for a wide range of uses. If budget is no issue, this would be my pick

Radiomaster Zorro

Price: $79.99

TX: 4in1/CC2500/ELRS

Gimbals: Hall

Weight: 350g

What seems to be the new value king, this radio has a ton of things going for it. Integrated ELRS in a (hopefully) good radio will definetly be a first. The other TX options are also nice, and the externally adjustable gimbals will be nice for easy customization... when it comes out.
As of the time of me writing this (22.12.2021, talk about rushing before XMas), it's on pre-order

Eachine TX16S

Price: $152.99

TX: 4in1

Gimbals: Hall

Weight: 736g

What seems to be a colaboration between Eachine and Radiomaster, they made a surprisingly good radio. Externally adjustable hall gimbals, pretty much the same feature set as the TX16S, but with a price that is much closer to when the OG TX16S first came out. Less available, but the price may make up for that

BetaFPV LiteRadio 2 SE/3

Price: $44

TX: CC2500

Gimbals: Potentiometer

Weight: 225g

Keeping these together as they're almost the same radio. The 3 brings better gimbals, slightly different switches, and a fully fledged configurator. The 2 SE used to be pretty bad, but it will be pretty much the same as the 3 in orders made after December

TBS Tango 2

Price: $159.99

TX: Crossfire

Gimbals: Hall

Weight: 516g

If you want a smaller, more compact radio with top end specs, this the one to go with. Integrated crossfire for long range, and a built-in battery make it very easy to start with. TBS make good quality stuff, and if you're used used to a game controller, this might be the best pick

TBS Mambo

Price: $139.99

TX: Tracer

Gimbals: Hall

Weight: 565g

Very close to the tx12, it's a smaller form factor, much like the tango. It's slightly cheaper, runs Tracer internally, and the antenna is internal as well, making it hard to break, and look cleaner
