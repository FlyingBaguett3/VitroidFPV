Everything to do with video transmission and reception

Goggles, video receiver modules, VTX and VRX antennas, cameras, and other misc. accessories, basically everything related to video. If you're trying to decide, or don't even have any prior experience, this list will hopefully help you!
Prices may depend on where and when you source the parts, (prices and specs are sourced from the manufacturers website when possible)

Goggles

It comes without question that without goggles, you won't be able to fly FPV. These are some good picks from budget to high-end, from box goggles to slimlines

Eachine EV800D

Price: $88

Screen: LCD 800x420

Receiver: Diversity

Weight: 362g

For a long time, these have been THE best budget goggles out there. For the price, they still hold up. Even though this is eachine, they work well, and for what you pay, you won't get that much more

Eachine EV800DM

Price: $92

Screen: LCD 900x600

Receiver: Diversity

Weight: 320g

A newer revision of the EV800D, these have some better features, such as focal adjustment, and better fit for glasses. However the screen is not detatchable, and the battery is smaller

Skyzone Cobra S

Price: $140

Screen: LCD 800x480

Receiver: RapidMix

Weight: 332g

These are technically made by Skyzone and Eachine, but Eachine can just slap their name on anything, these look to be made purely by skyzone. Great option if you want better reception, space for glasses, and a more modern design. They can also fit a full VRX module if you want to upgrade them in the future

Skyzone Cobra X

Price: $220

Screen: LCD 1280x720

Receiver: RapidMix

Weight: 332g

An improved version of the Cobra S, these have better DVR, a higher screen resolution, and can switch aspect ratios. Other than that they are all very similar

Skyzone 02O

Price: $330

Screen: OLED 640*400

Receiver: SteadyView

Weight: 210g

Updated from the popular 02C which were great value back then. Better OLED displays, better VRX (as far as I know, it's the same as RapidMix), and they're about the cheapest slimlines worth getting nowadays

Skyzone 04L

Price: $399

Screen: LCOS 1280x960

Receiver: SteadyView

Weight: 267g

Even if they're cheaper than the full high-end O4X, you don't lose much. LCOS displayes are really close to OLED, same receiver, same resolution, but you only get 30FPS for DVR

Eachine EV300o

Price: $499

Screen: OLED 1024x768

Receiver: RapidMix

Weight: 262g

Another "shared" venture of eachine and skyzone, these are very similar to the O4X, except for a lower resolution and 30FPS DVR

Skyzone O4X

Price: $529

Screen: OLED 1200x960

Receiver: SteadyView

Weight: 267g

Considered by many to be THE high-end analog goggles out there, they are pretty much the best you can get without going to DJI. The DVR goes up to 60FPS, and you have better screens

Fatshark Attitude V6

Price: $350

Screen: LCOS 1280x960

Receiver: Diversity

Weight: 199g

Fatshark has been making FPV goggles for ages, but has lately fallen out of favor. These goggles still hold their ground though, mainly with the use for the SharkByte digital system, favored by racers

DJI FPV Goggles V1/V2

Price: $529-$569 (nice)

Screen: OLED 1440x810

Receiver: DJI FPV

Weight: 420g

Your only choice if you want to go DJI digital. There is very little in terms of useful differences between the V1/V2, so I'm keeping them together. The V2 come with a battery, and can support the DJI FPV drone (would not recommend though)

VRX Modules

If your goggles don't come with a module installed, if you want to upgrade, or if you want to use analog with DJI, these are what you might want to look at

TBS Fusion

Price: $119.95

RF Sensitivity: -96dBm

Weight: 30.5g

In collaboration with AchilesFPV and BrainFPV, this module promises to be the best module out there. And from what I've seen, it fully well may be. It has tons of features, and also integration into other TBS systems. Chepaer than RapidFire, it might the better option for some

ImmersionRC RapidFire

Price: $149

RF Sensitivity: -96dBm

Weight: 68g

Another great offering, IRC make great video related products. Some people say that RapidFire is better than Fusion, others the other way around. What I can say is that both of these modules will work great to give you good reception all-round

AKK Diversity RX

Price: $34.99

RF Sensitivity: -93dBm

These two modules work decently well by their own, but when flashed with custom firmware (Achiles) they hold their ground to the big names. For the price it's a great option if you don't mind the slightly hacky upgrades

Eachine Pro58

Price: $28.99

RF Sensitivity: -92dBm

A slightly cheaper option to go with the AKK VRX. You can also flash it with Achiles to get very good performance. It's also more available, so you have a better chance of finding it outside of banggood

Foxeer WildFire

Price: $88.99

RF Sensitivity: -98dBm

Pretty good middle ground if you don't want to drop that much money, but still want to get something that works well out of the box. Foxeer have made some great video procucts, this one should be no different

FatShark Shark Byte RX5.1

Price: $249.99

RF Sensitivity: -101dBm

Weight: 80.3g

If you want to go with SharkByte, you will need this VRX to be able to receive the video. It's a middle ground digital system, with low latency comparable to analog, and better video quality than analog. But the quality is worse than DJI. There have been some controversies between HDZero and FatShark, so maybe expect some stuff to change

Cameras

Yet another part that's kinda needed to fly FPV. In all prices, kinds, and sizes, from tinywhoop cams, to big full-size cams.
There are size standards to keep in mind. Full-size (28x28), Mini (21x21), Micro (19x19), Nano (14x14), and Pico (12x12). Some cams go out of that spec (the original DJI cam is 20x20)
For some cams that come in multiple sizes, the price will be listed as a range from the cheapest to the most expensive size

Foxeer Razer

Price: $17.99-$19.99

Resolution: 1200TVL

Scale: 4:3/16:9

Size: Mini/Micro/Nano/Pico

One of the best priced cameras out there, for the price there isn't much that can beat it. It's nothing special, but it works great as a general budget cam

Foxeer Toothless 2

Price: $29.9-$36.9

Resolution: 1200TVL

Scale: 4:3/16:9

Size: Mini/Micro/Nano

With the bigger 1/2" sensor, it can capture more light, and so it gives really good video. The starlight variant is pretty good for low-light situations too

Foxeer Cat 3

Price: $39.9-$42.9

Resolution: 1200TVL

Scale: 4:3/16:9

Size: Mini/Micro

Made to excel in very low light situations, it will be great for night flying. There's also an IR sensitive variant for an even better low-light performance

Foxeer T-Rex

Price: $44.9

Resolution: 1500TVL

Scale: 4:3/16:9

Size: Mini/Micro

A great option if you want some of the highest quality of analog. This came is made for racers and freestyle alike, and probably one of the only cams with 1500TVL

Caddx Ratel 2

Price: $30.99

Resolution: 1200TVL

Scale: 4:3/16:9

Size: Micro

With a sensor even slightly bigger than the Toothless 2 (1/1.8") it is considered by many to be the best all-round cam. It's durable, and available in lots of stores

Caddx Ant

Price: $17.9

Resolution: 1200TVL

Scale: 4:3/16:9

Size: Nano

For the small crafts, where every gram matters. At only 2g, it will give great video at the weight and size, the Lite variant is about 0.3g lighter if you really want

Runcam Phoenix 2

Price: $29.99

Resolution: 1000TVL

Scale: 4:3/16:9

Size: Micro/Nano

Another great offering for video quality, also a big 1/2" sensor. There is even a special JB edition that has been set up to give the best video quality out of it

Runcam Nano 3

Price: $17.99

Resolution: 600TVL

Scale: 4:3

Size: Nano

At 1.1g, this is about the lightest you can get. You sacrifice video quality, but if you want to chase tenths of grams, this is the one to go with
