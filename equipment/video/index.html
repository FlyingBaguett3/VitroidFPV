Everything to do with video transmission and reception

Goggles, video receiver modules, VTX and VRX antennas, cameras, and other misc. accessories, basically everything related to video. If you're trying to decide, or don't even have any prior experience, this list will hopefully help you!

Goggles

Eachine EV800D

Price: $88

Screen: LCD 800x420

Receiver: Diversity

Weight: 362g

For a long time, these have been THE best budget goggles out there. For the price, they still hold up. Even though this is eachine, they work well, and for the price you won't get that much more

Eachine EV800DM

Price: $92

Screen: LCD 900x600

Receiver: Diversity

Weight: 320g

A newer revision of the EV800D, these have some better features, such as focal adjustment, and better fit for glasses. However the screen is not detatchable, and the battery is smaller

Skyzone Cobra S

Price: $140

Screen: LCD 800x480

Receiver: RapidMix

Weight: 332g

These are technically made by Skyzone and Eachine, but Eachine can just slap their name on anything, these look to be made purely by skyzone. Great option if you want better reception, space for glasses, and a more modern design. They can also fit a full VRX module if you want to upgrade them in the future

Skyzone Cobra X

Price: $220

Screen: LCD 1280x720

Receiver: RapidMix

Weight: 332g

An improved version of the Cobra S, these have better DVR, a higher screen resolution, and can switch aspect ratios. Other than that they are all very similar

Skyzone Cobra Lite

Price: $87

Screen: LCD 480x272

Receiver: Diversity

Weight: 330g

If you're tight on budget, but want a more modern option, these work pretty well. You lose resolution, and it's only a Diversity module. For the price, not too bad
